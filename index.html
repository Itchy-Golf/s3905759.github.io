Myer Briggs Results

My personal result for the Myer Briggs test was ENFJ or “The Protagonist”. Protagonists are leaders by nature, characterized often by their charisma and energy. People with the ENFJ personality type account for around 2% of the world’s population and are often those who work in positions of power out a longing to do good in the world. With their natural confidence and talent for influence, they find purpose in helping others around them while improving on themselves too. They are strong believers in the people and are unafraid to stand up for what they feel in right. Protagonists are authentic people who do their best to walk the walk and talk the talk, with some of their most valued moments leading others with contagious passion.

As long as the end goal is reached and everyone in their team is content in their roles, the protagonist is satisfied.

Project

Overview

Form Factor is an all encompassing health app that educates its modern users about the how’s and why’s of healthy nutrition and training. It’s a comprehensive application that provides overall food education such as calorie counts and macronutrient advice. Its value is then compounded by the addition of features like workout guides, supplement assistance and most importantly, custom generated food plans. These food plans will be presented after completion of the initial steps;

  1. Discovering macronutrients and calorie expenditure
  2. Understanding Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) and correct body composition for certain goals
  3. Selecting your ideal body composition
  4. Eliminating products the user does not wish to consume or dietary requirements

These prior steps will all be factored into recommending the user a calorie count and subsequent food plan to help them achieve their goals. They will also be provided training plan recommendations according to their body composition choice. Form Factor is in the fitness app category, however with its focus and necessity of nutritional and fitness learning, it distances itself from its possible counterparts that contain just a fraction of one of Form Factors features.

Motivation

Although the market for an app like Form Factor is somewhat saturated, the app itself will be a market first. It is differentiated by its educational value, combating common diet myths such as; “You can lose 10kg in 1 Month” and “Fad diets will help you lose weight” (Mukherjee and Mishra, 2015). Similar apps in the market such as Under Armour’s My Fitness Pal and My Diet Coach only dealt with its user on a superficial level; providing little personalised feedback and often they failed to assess the user’s prior knowledge of nutrition, if any (Davis et al., 2016). This has caused a dislike towards fitness apps among users seeking to change their body composition (Solbrig et al., 2017). The results delivered to the public via clinical and commercial weight loss programs are not desirable, as they produce weight loss but around 40% of users regain that weight (Tsai & Wadden, 2005). Users of these apps complain about not enough automation and a lack of motivation (Davis et al., 2016). Along with these negative factors, the majority of fitness apps lack evidence based designs, attempting to guess what features should be included (Tang, Abraham, Stamp & Greaves, 2014). Form Factor would aim to disrupt this market by providing sufficient motivation, knowledge and motivation to keep their users achieving goals with an app designed around functions who have purpose.

Description

Form Factor will be unique in the industry due to its level of nutritional education provided and it’s assistance in key areas of working out that for novices, can be quite difficult. These areas include but are not limited to, diet and training regimes. The goal of our app will be to ensure education is taken on board and learnt before any working out or dieting happens. To achieve this it will take the member through the prior mentioned steps below.

  1. Discovering macronutrients and calorie expenditure
  2. Understanding Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) and correct body composition for certain goals
  3. Selecting your ideal body composition
  4. Eliminating products the user does not wish to consume or dietary requirements

Beginning the user’s journey with education regarding what food consists of (macronutrients) and how these affect your body composition journey is foundational to a successful fitness journey. Without this prior knowledge, dieting has no ‘why’ behind it or understanding as to how dieting really works. This will then be accompanied by TDEE explanations, with the user selecting their ideal body composition following this education. This will help the application learn what the user wishes to look like in the future.

All of these educational steps will take form as interactive diagrams and dialogues with quick quizzes at the end of each section to ensure knowledge retention. If key questions are failed in this test, the app auto recommends going back over selected areas in need of improvement. Once the knowledge section is complete, the user can select food that’s don’t fit their dietary requirements from an array of images. This will help the app decide what food to include in their custom diet.

A custom diet will then be generated from a selection of foods and serving sizes to help the user achieve their outlined goal in the prior steps. The user will have the option to ‘refresh’ certain food items that they do not like. For example if the user is recommended a chicken salad for dinner however they would prefer another item, they can click the ‘refresh’ button and have a new food option cycled into their diet. They can do this action as many times as they wish to find a suitable replacement.

This feature not only solves the guess work that comes along with diet planning, it also means that the user will not have to continuously input their food into a calorie counting app, as is often a deterrent to consumers using these products (Davis et al., 2016). If the user wishes to have more variety, then a unique diet for each day can be saved for each day of the week.

Tools and Technologies

As Form Factor will be a two dimensional application, a key piece of software for its development will be Coco2D. Native app development will be key so the utilisation of cocos2d-swift and cocos2d-JS will be required for IOS and Android development respectably. As this project will be constructed by a team that is physically spread apart, the utilisation of Git will be key for development and team collaboration.

Skills Required

To achieve an app that is interactive and is partly gamified by design to maintain the user’s attention, Form Factor will require some intermediate skills in Objective C to be successful. However along with this, reasonable knowledge in Open-GI and Cocos2d will be required.

Outcome

If the project is successful then there will be frequent positive transformation stories returning to our team, letting us know how the changed their outlook on fitness. The original problems mentioned in the motivation behind this app will be solved within our community. Such as a lack of motivation or the abandonment of calorie counting apps due to them being too much work. We will be able to see an active fitness community who are learning the basics of health and fitness, taking on board their custom diet plans and changing them over time. This application will hopefully lead to further health education within the global fitness community, cutting through the clutter online of fads and myths, simplifying this exciting journey into something achievable for anyone taking it one step at a time.
